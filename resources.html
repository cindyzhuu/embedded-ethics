Ready-to-use ethics assignments and teaching materials for your AP Computer Science A classroom

Ethics Assignments

Four comprehensive ethics activities designed to integrate seamlessly into your AP CS A curriculum. Each assignment includes student handouts, teacher guides, and assessment rubrics.

Assignment 1: Data Privacy & Arrays

Beginner
2 class periods

Students explore data collection and privacy implications while working with arrays and data structures. Includes a case study on social media data collection.

CS Topics Covered:

  • Arrays and ArrayLists
  • Data storage and retrieval
  • Basic sorting algorithms

Ethics Focus:

  • Data privacy and consent
  • Responsible data collection
  • User rights and transparency

Assignment 2: Algorithmic Bias & Decision Making

Intermediate
3 class periods

Students examine how algorithms can perpetuate bias while learning about conditional statements and decision-making structures. Features real-world case studies.

CS Topics Covered:

  • Conditional statements
  • Boolean logic
  • Method design

Ethics Focus:

  • Algorithmic bias
  • Fairness in automated decisions
  • Diversity in tech

Assignment 3: AI Ethics & Object-Oriented Programming

Advanced
4 class periods

Students design an AI system while considering ethical implications. Connects OOP concepts with responsible AI development principles.

CS Topics Covered:

  • Classes and objects
  • Inheritance and polymorphism
  • Method overriding

Ethics Focus:

  • AI accountability
  • Transparency in algorithms
  • Ethical AI design

Assignment 4: Cybersecurity & Social Responsibility

Intermediate
3 class periods

Students explore cybersecurity ethics while learning about network security and data protection. Includes discussions of white-hat vs black-hat hacking.

CS Topics Covered:

  • Network security basics
  • Encryption and hashing
  • Input validation

Ethics Focus:

  • Responsible disclosure
  • Digital citizenship
  • Cybersecurity ethics
